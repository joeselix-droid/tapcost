TapCost Costing Playbook
Six levers built into the recommendation engine. Implement in order — Levers 1–3 typically close 80% of the gap to your 25% target.
01
Bleed Killers
Any keg over 35% best-case cost% gets flagged immediately. Reprice or cut. High-cost small kegs priced for half barrels are the most common culprit.
02
Under-Priced Volume
High-velocity beers priced $0.75–$1.50 below market. At 500+ pours/quarter, every $0.50 increase = $250+ recovered margin.
03
HH Restructure
2-for-1 doubles your effective cost%. Limit HH to kegs under $0.09/oz. For premium pours, switch to $1 off. Saves 3–5 cost points on your HH mix.
04
Discount Discipline
Track discount % per SKU — anything over 4% on a high-volume beer is a policy problem, not a pricing problem.
05
Upsell the Pint
16oz pours run 3–5 cost points better than 12oz on the same keg. Train staff to default to "Want a pint?" instead of "What size?"
06
Push Your Winners
Staff leads with beers under 22% cost that already sell well. Give servers three talking points and a free taste.
